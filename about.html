About BRSU

Learn about our organization, mission, and the team behind BRSU

Our Story

The Bangladesh Rohingya Student Union (BRSU) is a student-led movement committed to empowering Rohingya youth through education, advocacy, and unity. Founded by Rohingya students & youths from Myanmar in 20th June 2016, BRSU was born out of the urgent need to create a collective voice for Rohingya students who have been historically denied access to education, identity, and justice. We stand as a united front of young Rohingya scholars, leaders, and activists working to challenge marginalization, promote academic excellence, and lead efforts for community empowerment. Our work includes educational support, leadership training, mental health awareness, cultural preservation, and international advocacy. At BRSU, we believe that our identity is our strength. Through solidarity, self-determination, and education, we are building a new generation of Rohingya youth who are confident, informed, and ready to lead the way toward a just and inclusive future for our people. We are not just studentsâ€”we are advocates, bridge-builders, and the future of the Rohingya community..

Objective:

To empower Rohingya students and youth through inclusive education, leadership development, mental health support, and cultural preservation while actively advocating for the rights and justice on both local and global platforms. We aim to foster a generation of changemakers by creating safe, youth-led spaces for learning, dialogue, and community service. Through strong partnerships with local, regional, and international organizations, we strive to amplify Rohingya voices, build solidarity, and contribute to a more just and peaceful future for our people in Rakhine.

Today, BRSU serves hundreds of Rohingya students across Bangladesh, providing educational resources, mentorship programs, scholarship information, and community events. Our work is guided by the belief that education is a fundamental right and a powerful tool for positive change.

Our Mission

Our mission is to uplift Rohingya students and youth by expanding access to quality education, fostering leadership and civic engagement, promoting mental health and well-being, and preserving our cultural heritage. We are committed to grassroots organizing, global advocacy for a durable solution for genocide survivors, and community-driven action through meaningful partnershipsâ€”working to amplify Rohingya voices and create lasting impact for our people and future generations.
