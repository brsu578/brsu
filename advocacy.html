Advocacy

Amplifying Rohingya voices and advocating for justice, rights, and dignity

Our Advocacy Mission

BRSU's advocacy work is rooted in the belief that Rohingya youth must have a voice in decisions that affect their lives and future. We work to amplify Rohingya perspectives, challenge systemic discrimination, and advocate for policies that uphold our community's rights to education, citizenship, justice, and dignified return to our homeland.

Our advocacy is led by Rohingya students and youth who bring lived experience and authentic perspectives to policy discussions. We engage with local and international stakeholders, including governments, UN agencies, human rights organizations, and civil society groups to advance the rights and well-being of the Rohingya community.

Through research, public education, media engagement, and direct advocacy, we work to ensure that Rohingya voices are heard and that our community's needs and aspirations are reflected in policies and programs that affect us.

Key Advocacy Areas

Education Rights

We advocate for expanded access to quality education for Rohingya at all levels, from primary to higher education. This includes advocating for the recognition of Rohingya students' educational credentials, access to formal education in host countries, and scholarship opportunities.

Justice & Accountability

We support international justice mechanisms seeking accountability for crimes committed against the Rohingya. We document human rights violations, collect testimonies, and engage with legal processes to ensure justice for our community.

Citizenship & Identity

We advocate for the restoration of Rohingya citizenship in Myanmar and recognition of our identity and cultural rights. We challenge narratives that deny our existence and history as an indigenous ethnic group of Myanmar.

Safe & Dignified Return

We advocate for conditions that would enable the safe, voluntary, and dignified return of Rohingya refugees to their homeland in Myanmar, including guarantees of citizenship, security, and human rights protections.

Youth Participation

We advocate for meaningful inclusion of Rohingya youth in decision-making processes that affect our community. We work to ensure that young people's perspectives are represented in policy discussions, humanitarian response planning, and peace processes.

Policy Recommendations

Based on extensive consultation with our community and research on best practices, BRSU advocates for the following policy changes:

For the Government of Myanmar:

  • Restore full citizenship rights to the Rohingya population
  • End discriminatory policies and practices targeting Rohingya
  • Allow independent investigation of human rights violations
  • Create conditions for safe and dignified return of refugees
  • Ensure equal access to education for Rohingya students

For Host Countries:

  • Provide access to formal education for Rohingya children and youth
  • Recognize and accredit prior learning and qualifications
  • Allow Rohingya refugees to work and pursue higher education
  • Ensure protection and basic rights for Rohingya refugees
  • Include Rohingya representatives in decision-making processes

For International Community:

  • Support international justice mechanisms for crimes against Rohingya
  • Increase funding for education in humanitarian response
  • Create more scholarship opportunities for Rohingya students
  • Maintain pressure on Myanmar to address root causes of the crisis
  • Support Rohingya-led initiatives and organizations

Our Advocacy Campaigns

#EducationForAll Campaign

Our #EducationForAll campaign advocates for Rohingya students' right to access quality education at all levels. Through social media activism, policy briefs, and direct engagement with education authorities, we're working to remove barriers to education for Rohingya youth.

Launched: January 2023 Learn More →

#JusticeForRohingya Initiative

This campaign supports international justice mechanisms seeking accountability for crimes against Rohingya. We collect testimonies, raise awareness about ongoing legal processes, and advocate for comprehensive justice and reparations.

Launched: August 2022 Learn More →

#RohingyaVoices Media Project

The #RohingyaVoices project trains Rohingya youth in digital storytelling, journalism, and media advocacy. Through this initiative, young Rohingya are documenting their community's experiences, challenging misrepresentations, and sharing authentic stories with global audiences.

Our Advocacy Impact

Success Stories

UN Human Rights Council Resolution

BRSU representatives provided testimony that contributed to a UN Human Rights Council resolution calling for accountability for crimes against Rohingya and improved conditions in Rakhine State.

Education Policy Change in Cox's Bazar

Our advocacy led to a policy change allowing Rohingya students to access educational facilities in Cox's Bazar, benefiting over 500 students in the first year of implementation.

Scholarship Program Expansion

Following our campaign, a major international foundation expanded its scholarship program to specifically include Rohingya students, creating 25 new higher education opportunities annually.
